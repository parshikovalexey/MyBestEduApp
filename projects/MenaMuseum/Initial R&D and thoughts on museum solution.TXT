🛠️ How the "Hybrid Workflow" Works
Before (Digital): Teachers use the platform to introduce the museum. Students create the "Cover" and "Introduction" pages of their book, generating excitement and baseline knowledge.
During (Analog): The platform generates a Custom PDF Field Guide. Students print this, take it to the museum, and use pencils to sketch, take notes, or "collect" data in a tactile way.
After (Digital): Back at school, students upload photos of their sketches/notes into the WriteReader book. They transcribe their "analog" discoveries into "digital" stories, finalizing the book for display or printing.


🚀 The "Killer Feature" for Museums
The "Post-Visit Gallery"
Since students are finalizing their books after the visit, you can provide the museum with a live-updating "Community Impact Dashboard."
Museums can see (and show donors) exactly what students learned 2 weeks after they left.
This provides long-term "learning evidence" that is incredibly hard for museums to capture currently.
